Defense Ministry of United Republic of Chebodny
Nakhovny, Chebodny
Jan. 4, 1953

The following are accounts our agents recorded in the field the morning after 
the incident.

“The ground shook yesterday. After I got home from my shift at the foundry, the 
ground shook. I’ve never experienced anything like it before. My radio nearly 
fell off the counter. I ran outside to see what was going on and I couldn’t 
believe my eyes. The [Loryol Complex] had turned to dust. There was a second of 
silence that seemed to last forever before yells started coming from the rubble. 
I ran over to help. I’ll never forget the sight I saw when I got there.“

“I did what I could, everyone did. We felt powerless as we worked for hours 
clearing the rubble and accounting for our neighbors. What started as a frantic 
search and rescue turned into a grueling effort, especially with the night’s 
chill setting in. I had no choice but to leave.. I was too tired to be useful 
at that point.”

“I was walking home with my friend when we noticed the sky behind us flashed 
bright. We both turned around as fast as we could and we saw the complex 
falling. We don’t know what happened, there was no sound but the building 
collapsing. It was scary, so we ran back to my apartment.”
